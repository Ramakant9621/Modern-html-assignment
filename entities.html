what are html entities?List out 5 commonly used html entities

HTML entities are special codes used to represent characters that have a specific meaning in HTML, ensuring proper rendering and interpretation of the text. They are especially useful for characters that have special significance in HTML, such as reserved characters or characters with specific formatting meanings

Here are five commonly used HTML entities:

< - Less Than Sign (<):

Represents the opening angle bracket ("<") and is used to display the less-than sign without triggering HTML tag interpretation.

> - Greater Than Sign (>):

Represents the closing angle bracket (">") and is used to display the greater-than sign without triggering HTML tag interpretation.

& - Ampersand (&):

Represents the ampersand character ("&") and is used to display the ampersand without triggering HTML entity interpretation.

" - Double Quotation Mark ("):

Represents the double quotation mark (") and is used to display it without triggering attribute value interpretation. Useful in attribute values enclosed by double quotes.
